### 3.2 Research Questions

The following specific questions guide data collection and analysis:

### Behavior & Mental Models

- How do users currently [accomplish the task being researched] without our product?
- What language and terminology do users use when referring to [concept]?
- What triggers the need to [use the feature / complete the task]?

### Needs & Pain Points

- What are the most significant frustrations users experience with [current product / competitor]?
- What information or tools are missing from users' current workflow?
- What does success look like from the user's perspective?

### Usability & Interaction

- Can users complete [key task] without assistance within [X] minutes?
- At what point(s) do users experience confusion, hesitation, or errors?
- How do users interpret the [specific UI element / label / flow]?

## 4. Research Methodology

### 4.1 Selected Methods & Rationale

| Method | Type | Objective Covered | Rationale |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Stakeholder Interviews** | Qualitative | RO-01, RO-04 | Align on assumptions and known data before user research |
| **In-depth User Interviews** | Qualitative / Generative | RO-01, RO-04 | Explore mental models, context of use, and unmet needs |
| **Moderated Usability Testing** | Qualitative / Evaluative | RO-02, RO-03 | Observe task performance and identify friction in real-time |
| **Unmoderated Usability Testing** | Quantitative / Evaluative | RO-02, RO-05 | Scale task completion data across broader participant set |
| **Card Sorting (Open/Closed)** | Quantitative / Generative | RO-01 | Understand user mental models for information architecture |
| **Survey / Screener** | Quantitative | All | Recruit qualified participants and gather baseline attitudinal data |

### 4.2 Research Approach

- All sessions conducted remotely via **[Zoom / Lookback / UserTesting.com]** unless otherwise noted
- Sessions recorded (audio + video + screen share) with participant consent
- Think-aloud protocol used during all moderated sessions
- Observers may attend sessions silently — no interruptions without moderator approval
- Sessions conducted in **[Language]** — interpreter provided for [Language] sessions if applicable

## 8. Analysis & Synthesis Framework

### 8.1 Qualitative Analysis

- All session recordings transcribed verbatim (AI-assisted + manual QA)
- Affinity diagramming / thematic analysis in **[Miro / FigJam / Dovetail]**
- Insight tagging mapped to research objectives and research questions
- Severity rating for usability issues: **Critical / High / Medium / Low**

### 8.2 Quantitative Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Collection Method | Analysis Tool |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Task Completion Rate** | % of participants who completed the task without critical error | Observation log | Excel / SPSS |
| **Time on Task** | Elapsed time from task start to completion / abandon | Timer in session | Descriptive stats |
| **Error Rate** | # of errors per task per participant | Observation log | Descriptive stats |
| **System Usability Scale (SUS)** | Validated 10-item usability questionnaire (0–100 scale) | Post-test survey | SUS calculator |
| **Net Promoter Score (NPS)** | Likelihood to recommend (0–10 scale) | Post-session survey | NPS formula |
| **Findability Score** | % of participants who located key information without assistance | Observation log | Descriptive stats |

## 12. Appendix

### 12.1 Insight Severity Scale

| Severity | Definition | Action Required |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Critical** | Prevents task completion; user cannot proceed without assistance | Must fix before launch |
| **High** | Causes significant frustration or repeated errors; workaround exists but is non-obvious | Fix in current sprint |
| **Medium** | Causes confusion or slows the user down; workaround readily found | Prioritize in next sprint |
| **Low** | Minor inconvenience; easily worked around; cosmetic or preference-based | Backlog / nice-to-have |
